Overview

The Project

Flame Kabob is a Persian and Middle Eastern restaurant in Las Vegas. Despite having an online ordering website, the poorly designed interface meant users could not complete their orders successfully. We redesigned it into a trustworthy, visually appealing, and easy-to-use experience.

Discover

Understanding the Problem

Heuristic Evaluation

We began with a heuristic evaluation of the existing website, identifying key usability issues: no search bar, disorganized homepage, non-functional menu, poor color palette, and inconsistent typography.

User Interviews

We interviewed 12 users to discover what customers need when visiting a restaurant's website and what information they require to complete an order.

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed 8 ethnic restaurant websites for content and features.

Competitive analysis of ethnic restaurant websites
Define

Key Insights

Affinity Diagram

Organizing interview data through affinity mapping surfaced three core user needs: finding a restaurant they can rely on, thorough dish descriptions, and a photo for each food item.

Reflection

What I Learned

Through this project, which was one of my first as a UX student, I learned the significance of understanding users' needs throughout the design process. The most effective approach is by meticulously documenting and consistently referring back to all research findings at every stage of design.

Next step: Design a profile section where users can access previous orders and reorder with one click, and streamline the registration process to make it more user-friendly.
